Oceanographic Research

I was intoduced to data science through a subset of Oceanography called biogeochemistry. Biogeochemistry is a broad field involving anything from physiological responses on a molecular level to physical drivers on a planetary level. I use scientific computing to connect disparate datasets to analyze 15 years of Physical-biogeochemical trends off the west Antarctic Peninsula, build numerical models to leverage stable isotopes to trace Ocean currents in the Arctic, simulate seasonal Phytoplankton blooms, and design a controlled experiment to prepare for the costs of an increasingly acidic Ocean.
