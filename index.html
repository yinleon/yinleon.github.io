Oceanographic Research

I was intoduced to data science through a subset of Oceanography called biogeochemistry. Biogeochemistry is a broad field involving anything from physiological responses on a molecular level to physical drivers on a planetary level. I use scientific computing and data analysis to connect the gaps between these disciplines. Research in this field involves disparate and dirty data sets resulting in: analyzing 15 years of Physical-biogeochemical trends off the west Antarctic Peninsula, building numerical models to leverage stable isotopes to trace Ocean currents in the Arctic, simulating seasonal Phytoplankton blooms and designing experiments to prepare for the ecological and economic costs of an increasingly acidic Ocean.
